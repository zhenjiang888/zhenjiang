Principle of Programming Langauges

PRESENTATION GUIDE

Each of you will be expected to give two short (20-30 min.) presentations to the class: one on some sections of the textbook and one on an article from the programming languages literature. The topics and dates for your presentations will be assigned on April 17th (Monday).

You should use either OHP slides or PowerPoint for your presentation, and each presentation should last approximately 20 minutes. In addition, you should make enough copies of your slides for distribution at class.

In your textbook presentation, you should:

  • Explain the basic concepts with examples
  • Do an illustrative exercise in the textbook

In your article presentation, you should:

  • Give a brief biographical sketch of the author(s) of the article. (Several sentences is sufficient.)
  • Summarize and explain the main point or points of the article. Relate the points to the languages and ideas we have studied in class. Also try to place the article in its historical context:
    • Did the author(s) seem to have particular programming languages or paradigms in mind that they were critiquing or promoting? What languages existed when the article was written?
    • What languages were developed later that may have been influenced by the article?
