Principle of Programming Langauges

SYLLABUS

Course Overview:

The objectives of this course are

  1. to help you understand the structure and design principles of programming languages; and
  2. to develop your skills in describing, analyzing, and using the features of programming languages.

We will accomplish these goals by studying the concepts and paradigms that underlie programming languages and by discussing the design decisions and tradeoffs related to these concepts. We will also look at numerous examples of programming languages to see the historical progression of language design, and read articles from the programming languages literature.

This course will be run as a seminar course, which means that everyone will be actively involved in both teaching and learning from each other. You will:

  • Read sections of the textbook and give a presentation on it at class.
  • Read an article from the programming languages literature and give a presentation at class.
  • Explore language design issues through in-class discussions.

Assignments:

Each of you will be expected to give two short (20-30 min.) presentations to the class: one on some sections of the textbook and one on an article from the programming languages literature. These presentations will be distributed throughout the semester.

The topics and dates for your presentations will be assigned on April 17th (Monday). So you should do course registration by April 14th.

The format for the presentations is described in the Presentation Guide.
